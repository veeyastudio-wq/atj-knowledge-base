# ATJ TikTok Content Brief
*Last updated: June 2026. Canonical reference for all TikTok content decisions. Read this at the start of every TikTok content session.*

---

## Account
**Handle:** @withoutalawyer_uk
**Platform:** TikTok

---

## What this account is
Practical, plain English content for people navigating family court proceedings in England and Wales without a lawyer. It exists because the system is deliberately opaque, solicitors profit from that opacity, and nobody is cutting through it honestly for the people who need it most.

## What it is not
A legal advice account. A solicitor replacement. An emotional support channel. An American legal content account repurposed for the UK.

---

## Regulatory line — non negotiable
Never imply legal advice. Never tell someone what to do in their specific case. Explain what things mean, what the process involves, what options exist. The line is legal information, not legal advice. This applies to every single piece of content without exception.

---

## Visual style
Flat graphic illustration. Single object or partial figure focus. Bold solid background colour. Strong drop shadow. Minimal detail. Clean poster style composition. No texture, no painterly effects, no complexity. Colours vary per video — pastel, earthy, bold — but the illustration style stays consistent throughout. Motion added in CapCut — slow zoom, shadow grow, subtle animation. Never fully static.

---

## Production stack
- Midjourney for illustrations
- CapCut for motion, voiceover, and captions
- TikTok native publishing

---

## Tone of voice
Direct. Plain. A little defiant. The voice of someone who has been through it and refuses to pretend it's acceptable. Not angry for the sake of it — angry because real people are suffering real consequences from a system that was never designed to be navigable without expensive help. Never dramatic. Never wallowing. Acknowledge the problem, deliver the information, move on.

---

## The subtext that runs under every video
You are going through one of the hardest things a person can go through. You are going through it alone. That is not acceptable. And now there is something that can help.

---

## The villain
Not solicitors specifically. The opacity. The deliberate complexity. The system that profits from confusion. The framing is always: you deserve to understand what you're paying for, what you actually need a solicitor for, and what you can navigate yourself with the right information.

---

## Video structure
Three parts. Twenty seconds maximum. Roughly sixty spoken words.

- **Seconds 0 to 3** — the hook. One line. A scene or statement that makes the right person stop scrolling. Recognition, not melodrama.
- **Seconds 3 to 15** — the one thing. The single specific point they need to know. Precise, not general. UK specific always.
- **Seconds 15 to 20** — the close. One line. What to remember or what to do. Then the account name.

---

## The emotional hook principle
The first two to three seconds show a situation someone recognises instantly. Hands on a form. An unopened envelope. A clock. A phone screen at night. The image does the emotional work. The voice delivers the information.

---

## Content bank structure
The content maps the full journey of a case from first letter to final order. Three parallel tracks that many people do not realise run simultaneously.

- Track one — the divorce itself
- Track two — the financial remedy
- Track three — the child arrangements

The first video explains that these three tracks exist and run in parallel. Everything else builds from there.

---

## Cadence
Once a week minimum. Batch produced monthly — four to five videos in one session to maintain a buffer. If a video performs strongly, post more that week while the algorithm is paying attention.

---

## The product connection
Never a hard sell. The content creates the feeling of "I wish I had something like this." The close of select videos references that something now exists — but only once the product is live. Until then, build the audience. When ready, one line at the end of select videos: "if you want someone to walk through this with you, link in bio." The link in bio points to the product URL.
