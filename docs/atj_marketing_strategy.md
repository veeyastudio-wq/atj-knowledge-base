# ATJ Marketing Strategy
*Last updated: June 2026. This is the canonical reference for ATJ's marketing strategy. All active threads should read this at the start of each session and update it with any new decisions or progress before closing.*

---

## Product assumption
This strategy assumes the product is fully built and ready for launch. Active development and research threads run in parallel and are tracked separately in project_state.md.

---

## Pricing
- 15-day free trial
- £35 per month after trial
- Value anchor: what a solicitor would typically charge for the same task, not an affordability argument
- Note: £35 is a working assumption, not yet tested with real users

---

## Channels

### 1. Google Search Ads
- Target people actively searching for help with family court, litigant in person, self-representing
- Why search: captures existing intent rather than interrupting a scroll
- Why this niche: solicitors don't bid on these terms, so competition and cost should be lower than typical legal advertising
- Points to coming soon or waitlist page before pilot completes, switches to live subscription page after pilot clears

## TikTok
Full TikTok content brief, account details, visual system, production stack, cadence, and all 20 scripts are documented in docs/tiktok_content_brief.md. Read that file at the start of every TikTok content session. Do not duplicate content here.

### 3. SEO Blog Content
- Vilam's name attached as founder on all written content
- Why: Google explicitly wants legal content tied to a real, accountable person
- Strategy: target long-tail, specific questions the big sites don't cover well
- Do not compete head-on with Wikivorce, Citizens Advice, Rights of Women, Resolution on broad terms
- TikTok scripts repurposed into blog posts, double use of the same content

### 4. Borrowed Authority
- Pursue links and mentions from Wikivorce, Citizens Advice, Resolution, FFRAP, Support Through Court, McKenzie Friends
- A link from a trusted site builds Google's trust faster than organic ranking alone
- Connects to outreach already running in parallel threads

---

## Channels ruled out and why
- **LinkedIn**: six months of real effort on a previous product produced nothing, diagnosed as a distribution problem, new accounts get almost no organic reach regardless of content quality
- **Facebook Ads**: ruled out in favour of fresh search intent via Google
- **AI avatar or synthetic presenter**: removes real accountability, research shows AI avatar accounts rarely break through, and it undercuts the trust positioning
- **Personal trauma storytelling to cold audiences**: Vilam is not comfortable broadcasting private experience to strangers, and rightly so. A real name and founding reason is sufficient and sits on the product itself, not in cold-traffic content
- **Higher ad budget (£500 to £1,000/month)**: not realistic at this stage

---

## Trust and human signature
- ATJ should not be anonymous end to end
- Vilam's name, founding reason, and why he built it lives on the product itself, visible before and after signup, ongoing and constant, not a one-off bio page
- This is not a personal trauma narrative for cold strangers, it is real accountability attached to something real
- The solicitor review, once complete, should be named and visible on the product as a trust signal

---

## Timing and sequencing
1. TikTok content and SEO blog content start now, building in the background
2. Search ads start pointing to a coming soon or waitlist page
3. Pilot runs, small and closely watched, even five to ten people guided personally
4. Full subscription page goes live, search ads switch to point there
5. Marketing tap opens fully once pilot has cleared

*If no research participants are found through outreach, the pilot itself absorbs that function. The pilot becomes the first real-world test, not a replacement for discovery but a legitimate fallback.*

---

## Regulatory guardrails that apply to all content
- Never answer "what should I do in my specific situation", only explain what something means or involves
- No claims about what the product can do that have not been tested yet
- The solicitor comparison in pricing copy must be directionally true, not a precise figure that cannot be backed up
- The legal information versus legal advice line applies to all content, not just the product itself

---

## What is still open and unresolved
- £35 price point not yet tested with real users
- Whether the 15-day trial should trigger around a real case moment rather than a flat calendar window
- No tracking or measurement set up yet, needs to be in place before any spend goes live
- TikTok posting cadence and realistic monthly output not yet decided
- Solicitor review of system_prompt.md still pending, this is also the thing that enables legal credibility claims in SEO content

## Thread log: Citizens Advice outreach

### Goal
Find people who went through family court proceedings with zero legal representation throughout — the one research population not yet captured in any conversation. Individual Citizens Advice bureaux help people complete forms and navigate proceedings day to day, putting them in direct contact with this population.

### Approach decided
- Target individual London bureaux, not the national organisation
- The ask is a referral relationship, not client data. Bureaux cannot share client information so the email asks for a short call to explore whether they would pass on an invitation to clients who might want to share their experience

## Thread log: McKenzie Friends outreach

### Competition concern and resolution
Vilam raised two concerns: (1) ATJ might displace McKenzie Friend work, and (2) using their goodwill to build something that eventually competes with them felt ethically off.

Resolution reached in this thread:
- On displacement: McKenzie Friends physically attend court, advocate in real time, and maintain months-long relationships. ATJ handles comprehension, process guidance and grounding between hearings. Overlap exists only at the lightest end of what a McKenzie Friend does. ATJ may free them up for cases that genuinely need a human present. Displacement is real but partial
- On transparency: the fix is honesty, not avoidance. Approach McKenzie Friends openly about what ATJ is and let them decide. The test is whether they would feel misled reading the outreach email after the fact. If no, proceed
- Decision: pursue McKenzie Friends as a research channel with transparent framing
