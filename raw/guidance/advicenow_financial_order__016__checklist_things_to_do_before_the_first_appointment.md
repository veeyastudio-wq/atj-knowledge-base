# Checklist - things to do before the first appointment

There are quite a few other documents you need to fill in or get and then send to the court and your ex before the first appointment. Some documents need to be agreed with your ex, if at all possible. Depending on your particular situation, you may not need to do all of them.

Below is a checklist of all the tasks you need to do. Buy our extended guide for more detailed step-by-step help on all of these tasks.

- If you have a mortgage, send a copy of your application (Form A) and the notice of first appointment (Form C) to the bank or building society that lent you the money. Later you will need to tell the court you have done this – this is called ‘Confirmation of service’.
- If you have a pension, look at Pensions and divorce to find out how to get information on any state and private pensions you may be entitled to.
- Fill in Form E and collect together the evidence to send in with it. Buy our extended guide for detailed information about how to fill in your Form E.
- Fill in the ES1 document and try and agree what you put in it with your ex.
- Fill in the ES2 schedule and try and agree what you put in it with your ex.
- If you own the family home, get a valuation from a local estate agent and try to agree this with your ex. If you cannot agree it can be helpful to get three and agree on the average value.
- Get three sets of information on possible properties (from Rightmove, Zoola etc.) that would meet your future housing needs and then another three that you say would meet your ex’s future housing needs. Where any children will live or spend time will have an impact on your housing needs.
- Get information from a mortgage adviser about your borrowing capacity in the future (from someone you and your ex can agree on asking together, if possible)
- Fill in Form G - find out more about this in our extended guide .
- Prepare a Questionnaire - depending on what your ex says in their Form E. Buy our extended guide to find out more about how to do this and see an example.
- Prepare a position statement – this is a short statement where you set out what your views are of the case and what orders you would like the court to make. See an example of this in our extended guide .
- If the court asks you to prepare a 'bundle' you need to do this. The bundle is a file of the most important documents in a court case. It can be in paper or electronic format. Find out more about how to do this and see an example in our extended guide . The rules on bundles are changing on 2nd March. We will update our guide as soon as we can. If you buy our extended guide before we update it, leave your email address in this short form and we will send you a free digital copy of the updated guide when it is ready.
- Fill in Form H - this about costs.
- Fill in Form FM5 about your efforts to try and settle your dispute out of court. (You do not have to do this form if you are entitled not to go to a MIAM due to domestic abuse).

Send these forms to the court (and copies to your ex) within the deadline given on Form C.

If you can afford it, it will be very helpful to get some legal advice on how to prepare for the first appointment, in particular your ES1 summary, ES2 schedule, position statement and questionnaire. The court might also ask you to do a chronology. In order to access the advice at this reduced price you need to have read all the guidance in our extended guide (available to buy below).
