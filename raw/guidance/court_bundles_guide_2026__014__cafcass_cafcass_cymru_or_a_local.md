If a document is not in English and the court has directed that it must be relied on, a
certified English translation may also need to be included in the bundle. Further
information about translation and interpreting arrangements in the courts is available via
the HMCTS website – Get an interpreter at a court or tribunal - GOV.UK
Documents that you should not include in the bundle unless ordered to do so.
• Correspondence (This means letters sent between you and the other people in the
case including lawyers and any letters sent to experts.)
• Copies of emails, text messages, WhatsApp messages or any form of social media
communications
• Voice notes or other recordings3
• Bank or credit card statements and other financial documents
• Notes of contact visits
• Foster carer logs
• Photographs4
• Documents that were only used to discuss a possible agreement.
