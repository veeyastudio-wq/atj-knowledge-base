# Key terms

• A procedural hearing is a short hearing about how a case will be managed.
For example, the judge may deal with timetables, directions or what steps need
to happen next. Bundles for these hearings usually require fewer documents
than are needed for other types of hearings.
• A fact-finding hearing is where the judge hears evidence when there is a
dispute about the facts. The judge then decides what did or did not happen.
These hearings might be needed, for example, where there are allegations of
domestic abuse. These hearings usually require more documents and evidence
to be included in a bundle.
• A final hearing is the hearing when the judge makes the final decision about
how the application should be dealt with. For example, the judge might make a
child arrangements order setting out who a child should live with or spend time
with.
• A Financial Dispute Resolution hearing only takes place in cases involving
money, for example when separating or divorcing couples ask the court to help
sort out their financial arrangements. It is usually used when a separating or
divorcing couple cannot agree on how to sort out their finances, such as:
property, savings, pensions and debts. The judge at a Financial Dispute
