How court orders work
The person who is asking for a court
order against someone is called the
‘applicant’.
The person who the court order is
against is called the ‘respondent’.
The applicant will fill in some court
documents to explain why they want
a court order.
The respondent will be told that the
applicant has asked for a court order
against them.
They will be given a copy of the court
