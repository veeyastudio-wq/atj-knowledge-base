# What is the First Appointment (FDA)?

## What it is

The First Appointment (FDA) is a short directions hearing — typically 30 to 45 minutes — that takes place after both parties have exchanged their Form E financial statements. It is a procedural hearing, not a substantive one: the judge's role is to identify the issues in dispute, ensure that financial disclosure is adequate, and give directions for what must happen before the case can proceed to the Financial Dispute Resolution hearing (FDR). Neither party argues the merits of their financial case at the FDA.

## When it happens

The FDA is Stage 4 of the financial remedy track. It takes place after both parties have filed and exchanged Form E (the detailed financial statement required in Stage 3). The court sets the FDA date when Form A is filed; the date is usually around 12 to 16 weeks after the Form A is issued. The FDA comes before the FDR hearing (Stage 5) and before any final hearing (Stage 6).

## What it involves

Before the FDA, each party must file and serve on the other party a set of standard documents, usually no later than 14 days before the hearing:

- **A chronology** — a concise timeline of the key events of the marriage and the parties' financial history.
- **A questionnaire** — a list of requests for further documents or information from the other party, arising out of their Form E. Questionnaires must be proportionate; the judge will decide which questions must be answered.
- **A schedule of issues** — a summary of the matters that remain in dispute between the parties.
- **Form G** — a form on which each party states whether they are in a position to use the FDA as an FDR (see below).

At the FDA, the judge considers the Form E statements, the questionnaires, and the schedule of issues. The judge then decides:

- Which of the questionnaire requests must be answered, and by when.
- Whether any further valuations, reports, or expert evidence are needed (for example, property valuations or pension reports).
- Whether the case is suitable to be dealt with as an FDR at the next hearing.
- The timetable toward the FDR or final hearing.

In some cases, if both parties confirm they are ready (via Form G) and agree, the FDA and FDR can be combined into a single hearing. This is less common where the issues are complex.

## What to know if you're unrepresented

**The FDA is not the time to argue the case.** An unrepresented party who arrives expecting to present their financial position and persuade the judge will be caught off guard. The judge's task at the FDA is case management, not adjudication. Arguments about the merits of who should receive what are not heard at this stage.

**The documents filed before the FDA matter.** The chronology, questionnaire, and schedule of issues shape everything that follows. A poorly drafted questionnaire — asking for irrelevant documents or omitting important requests — cannot easily be remedied later. The judge will scrutinise questionnaire requests for proportionality and may disallow requests that are excessive or irrelevant.

**Form G must be completed.** Each party must confirm whether they are in a position to proceed to an FDR at the next hearing. Filing Form G incorrectly or not understanding its purpose can cause confusion at the hearing.

**Disclosure gaps will be addressed here.** If the other party's Form E is incomplete or inadequate, the FDA is the appropriate time to raise this through the questionnaire process — not to make oral complaints at the hearing. Specific, targeted requests for missing documents are more effective than general objections.

**The judge's tone is neutral.** At the FDA the judge is not indicating which party has the stronger case. Questions from the judge about a party's financial position are part of case management, not a signal of the judge's sympathies.

## Key terms

**Form E** — the detailed financial statement each party must complete and exchange before the FDA, setting out all assets, income, liabilities, pensions, and outgoings.

**Form A** — the form filed to start financial remedy court proceedings.

**Form G** — a form on which each party confirms whether they are ready and willing to treat the FDA as an FDR (combined hearing).

**Chronology** — a concise document setting out the key dates in the parties' marriage and financial history, required before the FDA.

**Questionnaire** — a list of requests for further documents or information from the other party, arising out of their Form E; submitted to the court and the other party before the FDA.

**Schedule of issues** — a document summarising the matters that remain in dispute between the parties.

**Directions** — the judge's formal instructions setting out what each party must do and by when before the next hearing.

**FDR** — Financial Dispute Resolution hearing; the settlement-focused hearing that typically follows the FDA.
