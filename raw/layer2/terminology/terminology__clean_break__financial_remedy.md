# Clean break

## What it means

A clean break is a financial settlement that ends all financial claims between the parties permanently, with no ongoing payments between them. Once a clean break order is made, neither party can return to court to make further financial claims against the other — even if circumstances change significantly in the future. The court is required by the Matrimonial Causes Act 1973 to consider whether a clean break is appropriate in every financial remedy case.

## When you will encounter it

In financial remedy proceedings, at every stage from the FDA onwards. The court will ask whether a clean break is possible. If one party is seeking ongoing maintenance payments and the other wants a clean break, this will be one of the issues at the FDR and final hearing.
