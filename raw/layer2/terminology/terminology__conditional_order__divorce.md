# Conditional order

## What it means

The conditional order is the first of the two court orders that make up a divorce. It is the court's confirmation that the legal requirements for divorce have been met and that there is no reason the divorce should not proceed. It does not end the marriage — the marriage ends with the final order. At least six weeks must pass between the conditional order and the final order.

## When you will encounter it

After the 20-week cooling-off period, once you have applied for the conditional order and the court has considered it. The court will set a date on which the conditional order is pronounced. You will receive a certificate confirming it has been made.
