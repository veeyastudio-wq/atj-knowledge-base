# Tipstaff

## What it means

The Tipstaff is the enforcement officer of the High Court of Justice, based at the Royal Courts of Justice in London. There is only one Tipstaff, supported by a deputy and assistants. The Tipstaff is not a bailiff and not a police officer — they are a dedicated court official whose authority comes directly from orders made by the High Court. When a High Court order is directed to the Tipstaff, the Tipstaff can authorise a police officer, a bailiff, or another person to carry out the order on their behalf throughout England and Wales.

## When you will encounter it

The Tipstaff becomes involved when the High Court makes an enforcement order in urgent or serious family cases. This most commonly happens in child abduction cases, but also arises in cases involving forced marriage, female genital mutilation prevention orders, and wardship. The types of orders directed to the Tipstaff include:

- **Collection orders** — directing the Tipstaff to locate and take a child into protective custody
- **Location orders** — directing the Tipstaff to find a respondent and seize their passport or other travel documents
- **Passport orders** — directing the Tipstaff to seize a specific person's passport
- **Bench warrants** — directing the Tipstaff to arrest a named person and bring them before the court

If a court order names the Tipstaff, that order carries the full authority of the High Court. The Tipstaff, their deputies, or any police officer acting on their behalf is authorised to enter premises — by force if necessary — to execute the order. Any person who obstructs a Tipstaff order risks being in contempt of court.

If you receive such an order or a Tipstaff attends your address, you must comply. You have the right to seek legal advice after any arrest, but that right does not allow you to delay or refuse compliance with the order itself.

**Port alert orders** are a related but separate power. A port alert directs UK Border Force to stop a named person (or a child) leaving the country. These can be made with or without a Tipstaff direction. A freestanding port alert order goes directly to Border Force without involving the Tipstaff at all.

## Track

Child arrangements (most common); also applies in forced marriage, FGM prevention, and wardship cases.
