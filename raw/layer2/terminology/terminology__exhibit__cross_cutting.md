# Exhibit

## What it means

An exhibit is a document or item of evidence that is attached to a witness statement and formally referred to within it. Each exhibit is given a reference label — typically the initials of the person making the statement followed by a number, such as AB1, AB2. The witness statement refers to the document by that label ("I refer to the email dated 3 January 2025, exhibited at AB1") and the exhibit is attached behind the statement in the bundle.

## When you will encounter it

When you prepare or receive a witness statement. Any document you refer to in your statement — a letter, an email, a bank statement, a photograph — must be exhibited. You will also encounter exhibits in the other party's witness statement and in Cafcass reports.

## Track

Cross-cutting — applies to all tracks.
