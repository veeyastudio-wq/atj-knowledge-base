# Statement of truth

## What it means

A statement of truth is a declaration signed at the end of certain court documents — witness statements, financial statements, and some applications — confirming that the contents are true to the best of the signatory's knowledge and belief. Signing a statement of truth on a document that contains false information is contempt of court, which is a criminal offence.

## When you will encounter it

On your witness statement, on Form E (the financial disclosure form), and on certain other documents the court requires you to sign. The wording is prescribed — it must follow the exact form set out in the Family Procedure Rules.
