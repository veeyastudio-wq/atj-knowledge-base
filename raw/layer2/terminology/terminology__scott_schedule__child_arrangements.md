# Scott Schedule

## What it means

A Scott Schedule is a structured table used in family court proceedings to set out allegations clearly, typically allegations of domestic abuse or harmful behaviour, alongside the other party's responses to each. It lists allegations row by row, with columns for the allegation, the date, supporting evidence, and the respondent's reply. Its purpose is to help the court identify exactly what is in dispute before a fact-finding hearing.

## When you will encounter it

If domestic abuse or harmful behaviour is alleged in child arrangements proceedings, the court may direct one party to prepare a Scott Schedule ahead of a fact-finding hearing. Note that following Re H-N and Others [2021] EWCA Civ 448, courts are required to consider whether a Scott Schedule is the most appropriate format — in cases involving patterns of coercive or controlling behaviour, narrative statements may be more suitable.

## Track

Child arrangements — most commonly in cases involving allegations of domestic abuse.
