Definitions

The Tipstaff is the enforcement officer of the High Court at the Royal Courts of Justice. They have a deputy and assistants and can authorise police officers to act on their behalf. Any obligation to give information to the Tipstaff or to hand over a document to them includes an obligation to do so to their deputy or assistant or a police officer acting on their behalf.

A [Collection] / [Location] / [Passport] Order is an order directed to the Tipstaff authorising them to take steps to [collect the child] / [locate the respondent and to thereafter seize any passport or other travel documents from them] / [seize any passport or other travel document held by the respondent as applicable].

Undertakings to the court by the solicitors for the applicant

The solicitors for the applicant undertake as follows:

To use any information provided to them by the Home Office solely to locate the respondent and thereafter to effect service of these proceedings upon them; and

To pay the reasonable costs incurred by the Home Office in complying with this order.

(where there are reasonable grounds to believe that a person to be served is residing in a refuge, it is not appropriate for the other party or their solicitor to have information relevant to their whereabouts disclosed to them as a result of a disclosure order. practitioners and the court must be alert to the guidance in re p (service on parent in a refuge) [2023] ewhc 471 (fam)), and the anticipated insertion of rule 6.23a fpr 2010)

IT IS ORDERED THAT:

The Home Office shall, by its officers or agents provide all information within its possession, custody or control relating to the whereabouts of the respondent [respondent(s) name(s) and date(s) of birth] and of the subject child[ren] [child(s) name and date(s) of birth]. Such information to be provided forthwith upon service of this order upon them to the solicitors for the applicant [applicant firm name].

The solicitors for the applicant have permission to serve a copy of this order by facsimile or by email if necessary

The Home Office are prohibited from informing the respondent of the making of this order or any action taken in compliance with it.

The reasonable costs incurred in compliance with this order are considered by the court to be a reasonable expense on the applicant’s legal aid certificate.

Costs are reserved.
