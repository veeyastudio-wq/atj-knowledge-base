# Practice Direction 9B – Communication of information from financial remedy proceedings

## PRACTICE DIRECTION 9B – COMMUNICATION OF INFORMATION FROM FINANCIAL REMEDY PROCEEDINGS


This Practice Direction supplements FPR rule 9.46


1.1


Subject to any direction of the court, information from financial remedy proceedings may be communicated for the purposes of the law relating to contempt of court in accordance with this Practice Direction.

### Onward communication


3.1 A person in the second column of the table at paragraph 2.1 may only communicate information relating to the proceedings received from a person in the first column for the purpose for which he or she received that information.

### Interpretation


4.1


In this Practice Direction- “lay adviser” means a non-professional person who gives lay advice on behalf of an organisation in the lay advice sector; and “McKenzie Friend” means any person permitted by the court or tribunal to sit beside an unrepresented litigant in court or in a tribunal to assist that litigant by prompting, taking notes and giving advice to that litigant.
